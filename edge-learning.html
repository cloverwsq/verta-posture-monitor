Edge Leaning Detected

Leaning at the edge of your chair puts strain on your lower back. Let's fix it!

üîç What's Happening?

When you lean at the edge of your chair, your lower back loses its natural support. This unstable position can lead to:

  • Lower back strain - Your spine is unsupported and muscles work overtime
  • Poor circulation - Pressure on the back of your thighs restricts blood flow
  • Hip flexor tightness - Extended sitting in this position shortens hip muscles
  • Increased fatigue - Unstable posture requires constant micro-adjustments

‚úÖ Quick Fixes

  • Scoot all the way back - Move your hips to the back of the chair until your back touches the backrest
  • Plant your feet flat - Both feet should be flat on the floor, hip-width apart
  • Use full back support - Press your lower back into the chair's backrest
  • Adjust chair height - Your thighs should be parallel to the floor when seated properly
  • Take standing breaks - Stand up every 30 minutes to reset your posture
